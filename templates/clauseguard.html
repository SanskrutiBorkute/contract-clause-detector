Security Deposit — Clause 3
CLAUSE 3 · SECURITY DEPOSIT · DETECTED RISK: HIGH
Security Deposit
The Tenant shall pay a security deposit of ₹1,20,000 (10 months rent). The Landlord may forfeit the entire deposit at his sole discretion for any damage, howsoever caused, including normal wear and tear. Deposit shall be returned within 180 days of vacating.
⚠ Problem 1 — Excessive Deposit Amount
The deposit is 10 months rent (₹1,20,000). Under Maharashtra Rent Control Act and standard practice, security deposit should not exceed 2–3 months rent (₹24,000–₹36,000). A 10-month deposit is legally contestable and financially exploitative.
⚠ Problem 2 — Forfeit for Normal Wear & Tear
The clause allows the landlord to forfeit your deposit for "normal wear and tear" — this is illegal. Normal wear and tear (paint fading, minor scuffs) is always the landlord's responsibility under Indian tenancy law. The tenant is only liable for wilful or negligent damage.
⚠ Problem 3 — 180-Day Return Period
A 180-day (6-month) return window for the deposit is unreasonable and unenforceable. Standard practice and model tenancy laws require deposit return within 30 days of vacating, after deduction for verified damages only.
✏ AI Suggested Rewrite
The Tenant shall pay a security deposit of ₹36,000 (3 months rent). The Landlord may deduct from the deposit only the cost of repairing damage caused by the Tenant beyond normal wear and tear, with written itemized proof. The remaining deposit shall be returned within 30 days of vacating, along with a written statement of any deductions.

What this means for you
You could lose up to ₹1,20,000 with no legal recourse if you sign this clause as written. The landlord can claim deposit for paint, scuffs, or any minor issue and you have no defined timeline to challenge it.
Indian Law Reference
This clause likely violates:

Maharashtra Rent Control Act, 1999
Model Tenancy Act, 2021 — Sec. 11
Consumer Protection Act, 2019
How to negotiate
Ask the landlord to change deposit to 2 months, add "excluding normal wear and tear" and reduce return window to 30 days. Most landlords in Nagpur will negotiate deposit down to 3 months if asked directly with reasons.
If landlord refuses
File a complaint with the Rent Controller, Nagpur or approach Consumer Forum after vacating. Keep all payment receipts and document the property condition with photos on move-in day.
